Introduction About Philippines

The Philippines is an archipelagic country in Southeast Asia, consisting of 7,641 islands. It is located in the western Pacific Ocean, bordered by the South China Sea to the west, the Philippine Sea to the east, and the Celebes Sea to the south. The country has a tropical climate, with rich biodiversity, beautiful beaches, and diverse ecosystems.

Basic Facts:

Capital: Manila

Largest City: Quezon City

Official Languages: Filipino and English

Population: Over 110 million (making it the 13th most populour country in the world)

Government: Unitar presidential constitutional republic

Currency: Philippine Peso (PHP)

Economy and Tourism:

The Philippine economy is one of the fastest-growing in Asia, driven by agriculture, services, remittances from overseas Filipino workers (OFWs), and the business process outsourcing (BPO) industry. The country is a top tourist destination, known for places like:

Boracay (white-sand beaches)

Palawan (crystal-clear waters and lagoons)

Bohol (Chocolate Hills and tarsiers)

Cebu (historical sites and diving spots)

Vigan (Spanish colonial town)
